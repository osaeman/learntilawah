The challenge

Struggling to get the pronunciation right?
Or reciting, but unsure it’s correct?

Many learners — young and old — start reading the Quran before the foundation is firmly in place: the Arabic letters, their points of articulation (Makharij), and the rules of Tajweed. Left unchecked, small slips in pronunciation quietly settle into habits that are hard to undo later.

Books, apps and audio recordings have their place, but none of them can hear you, correct you, or adapt to how you learn. On your own it is easy to soften a letter, shorten an elongation, or recite in a way that shifts the meaning — without ever realising it.

At Learn Tilawah, certified tutors take students through it step by step: live one-to-one classes, instant personal correction, and a clear, structured path from first letter to confident recitation.
